INTRODUCTION

This study shows preliminary results of the use of automatic learning (AdaBoost) for the prediction of type 2 diabetes using clinical information that includes anthropometric measurements and biochemical markers, as well as genotypes of eight single nucleotide polymorphisms (SNP). This study is framed in the context of the search for genetic polymorphisms that can be used for the prediction of type 2 diabetes in the Peruvian population. In addition, this study has allowed the development of a web tool that will allow the obtaining of clinical information for the realization of future predictions.
